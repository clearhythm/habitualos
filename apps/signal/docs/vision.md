# Signal — Core Vision

## The One-Liner

"Go find out if we should talk more."

## What This Means

Signal is the pre-conversation. It handles the part of networking that is awkward, repetitive, and inefficient — so that when two people actually talk, it's already worth both their time.

Your voice, available at scale, without you having to be present.

## The Experience

The chat feels like talking TO the candidate — first person, voiced by the behavioral record. Not a report. Not a screener. A conversation with someone who has unusual self-knowledge.

The fit score is delivered by the person:
> "My Signal score for this role is 84. That's based on 47 sessions — here's what drove it up and what held it back. Want to dig into any of it?"

The score becomes an invitation, not a filter. "Here's why I think we're aligned, here's where we might not be — now you decide if it's worth a real conversation."

## Why It's Different

Most candidates can't answer "where would you struggle in this role?" with any precision. Signal makes that possible — because the behavioral record is right there.

The agent is an instrument the candidate wields, not a separate voice. Like a doctor reading their own labs: they don't say "my diagnostic agent reports..." — they say "my numbers show X, here's what I think it means."
